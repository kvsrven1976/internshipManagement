About Vijayawada Division

Indian Railways is one of the largest rail networks in the world — the backbone of the nation’s transport and logistics. It connects major cities, ports, industrial hubs and agricultural belts, powering economic growth and mobility across India.

Vijayawada Division is the second largest division of South Central Railway, covering 1,068.194 route kilometres and 156 stations. Vijayawada Junction sits at the strategic tri-junction of the Chennai–New Delhi (North–South) and Chennai–Howrah (East–Coast) routes, with important branches to Guntur/Guntakal and Machilipatnam/Narsapur.

Ports & Economic Reach

The Division serves the East Coast ports of Krishnapatnam and Kakinada, supports surrounding industrial clusters, and caters to the vibrant agricultural economy of Coastal Andhra Pradesh.

About the IT Cell

The IT Cell was formed in 2020 to strengthen digital initiatives and improve operational efficiency. It started with 4 in-house employees and 4 interns, and is located at the DRM Office, near Vijayawada Railway Station. The Cell focuses on digital transformation, process automation and IT solutions for the Division’s operational and administrative requirements.
